Title: Product Positioning & Why Good Products Fail  
Link: https://youtu.be/O-bo48WTDfg  
Author: April Dunford  
Date: ~2 years ago  

Note: This video is relatively recent and reflects modern thinking on positioning, especially for SaaS and tech products.

---

Transcript:

This discussion focuses on product positioning and why many good products fail to gain traction.

On positioning:
Positioning is not about what your product is.

It is about:
- how customers understand it  
- how it fits into their world  
- why it matters to them  

On product failure:
Many companies believe:
- “we have a great product”  

But still fail because:
- customers don’t understand it  
- the value is unclear  
- it is placed in the wrong category  

A strong product alone is not enough.

On context:
Customers evaluate products based on context.

This includes:
- alternatives  
- competitors  
- existing solutions  

If the context is wrong:
- customers misunderstand the product  
- they compare it incorrectly  
- they don’t see its value  

On differentiation:
Positioning defines:
- who the product is for  
- what problem it solves  
- why it is better than alternatives  

Without clear differentiation:
- the product blends in  
- buyers cannot justify choosing it  

On target customer:
Choosing the right audience is critical.

A product may succeed or fail depending on:
- who you target  
- how well the problem fits their needs  

Positioning often improves by narrowing focus.

On messaging:
Good positioning leads to clear messaging.

Instead of:
- listing features  

Focus on:
- value  
- outcomes  
- use cases  

---

Key Insights:
- A great product can fail without clear positioning  
- Positioning is about customer perception, not product features  
- Context determines how customers evaluate your product  
- Narrowing your target audience improves clarity and traction  
- Clear differentiation is essential for growth
