Title: AI, Taste & Modern B2B Marketing Strategy  
Link: https://youtu.be/q4Gh4v7myKM  
Author: Dave Gerhardt  
Date: ~4 years ago  

Note: While this video is a few years old, the insights on brand, differentiation, and marketing strategy remain highly relevant, especially with the rise of AI tools.

---

Transcript:

This discussion focuses on the evolving role of AI and human judgment (“taste”) in modern marketing.

On marketing success:
Marketing success can be thought of as:

AI adoption × taste²

This means:
- adopting new tools like AI is important  
- but taste has a much larger impact  

On AI in marketing:
Right now, companies can gain an advantage by adopting AI faster than competitors.

However:
- over time, everyone will use the same tools  
- everyone will have access to similar prompts  
- technology will become standardized  

This removes differentiation from tools alone.

On the importance of taste:
Taste becomes the key differentiator.

Taste means:
- good judgment  
- understanding what resonates  
- knowing what “feels right” to the audience  

It is:
- hard to define  
- difficult to measure  
- impossible to fully document  

But it is obvious when done well.

On team building:
To succeed, companies should:

- hire people with strong taste  
- prioritize creative judgment  
- focus less on purely technical execution  

Even if leaders lack taste themselves, they should build teams that compensate for it.

On strategy:
The future of marketing will rely on:

- unscalable actions  
- creative decisions  
- human intuition  

These are things that:
- cannot be automated  
- cannot be easily written into processes  

On differentiation:
As tools become equal across companies:

- execution quality will matter more  
- creative thinking will matter more  
- brand and messaging will matter more  

---

Key Insights:
- AI adoption is important, but not a long-term differentiator  
- Taste (creative judgment) has an exponential impact on marketing success  
- As tools become commoditized, human creativity becomes the advantage  
- Great marketing teams prioritize taste over pure technical skills  
- The future of marketing relies on unscalable, human-driven decisions
