Post 1 (Demand Generation vs Lead Generation Shift)

Date: ~recent
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_marketing-demand-b2b-activity-7130189117371400192-y7RE 
Content:
Traditional B2B marketing is still stuck in a lead generation mindset.
The focus is on collecting emails, forms, and “MQLs” so Sales can follow up.
But modern buyers don’t behave that way anymore.
They:


research independently
avoid sales interaction early
consume content across multiple channels before ever converting
This makes lead-centric marketing inefficient.
A better approach is demand generation.
Instead of chasing leads, you focus on:


educating the market
building awareness in your ICP
creating demand before capture
attracting buyers when they are already problem-aware


The goal is not more leads.
The goal is more revenue from better-informed buyers who already want a solution.

Post 2 (Brand, Distribution & Dark Social Thinking)
Date: ~recent
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_marketing-advertising-linkedin-activity-7084881983880790017-uTpV 
Content:
Most B2B companies underestimate how modern buyers actually discover solutions.
They assume email and ads are the main drivers.
But in reality, discovery happens through:


LinkedIn content


podcasts


peer conversations


“dark social” sharing (DMs, private communities, word of mouth)


This changes how marketing should be built.
Instead of optimizing for short-term lead capture, companies should build a content distribution engine that consistently:


earns attention


builds trust over time


gets shared within buyer networks


stays visible throughout long buying cycles


The strongest marketing systems today behave like media companies, not campaign machines.
Attention and trust compound over time.
And distribution is the real competitive advantage.
