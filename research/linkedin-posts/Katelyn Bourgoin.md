Post 1 (Buying Happens at Trigger Moments)
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/katebour (profile content + trigger event framework)
Content:
People don’t buy because they see your marketing.
They buy when something in their life changes.
A “buying trigger” is the moment someone shifts from:


not looking for a solution
to

actively trying to fix a problem

These triggers are real-life events like:

hitting a revenue goal (or missing it)

a tool breaking or failing

a new responsibility or role

suddenly realizing current solutions aren’t working


Most marketers make the mistake of targeting demographics.
But the real leverage is in targeting moments.
If you understand what triggers buying behavior, you stop guessing.
You start showing up at exactly the right time.

Post 2 (Customers Don’t Say What They Will Do — They Show It)

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/katebour (behavioral psychology + buyer research framework)
Content:
What people say they will do is not reliable.
What people actually do is what matters.
Customers are often bad at predicting their own behavior because:
memory is biased
emotions change over time
people rationalize decisions after the fact
That’s why surveys alone are not enough.
To understand buyers, you need to study:
actual purchase behavior
real customer conversations
reviews and complaints
moments of friction before buying

The goal is not to ask people what they want.
The goal is to observe what made them act.
Because buying decisions are rarely logical.
They are triggered, emotional, and situational.
