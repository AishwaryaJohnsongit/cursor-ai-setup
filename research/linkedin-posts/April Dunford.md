Post 1 (Positioning is Context, Not Description)

Date: ~recent
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/aprildunford (post activity feed – positioning content)
Content:
Most companies think positioning is about describing what their product does.
But that’s not what actually makes a product win.
Positioning is about context.
It is how you shape the way people understand your product compared to alternatives.
When customers evaluate a solution, they are not asking:


what features does this have?
They are asking:
what should I compare this to?
why is this better than what I already use?
why should I care right now?


If you don’t control that frame, customers will default to comparing you against the wrong alternatives.
And that is where most products lose.
Good positioning makes it obvious:
who it is for
what problem it solves
what it should be compared against
why you are different
Without that clarity, even great products feel generic.

Post 2 (Positioning → Messaging → Sales Alignment)
Date: ~recent
Link: same profile
Content:
Positioning only works when it shows up in execution.
A lot of teams treat positioning as a document.
But positioning is only useful if it influences:

sales conversations
marketing messages
product decisions
customer understanding


The biggest failure point is the gap between strategy and execution.
Teams define positioning, but:
sales doesn’t use it
marketing interprets it differently
customers never experience it consistently


When that happens, positioning has no impact.
Strong companies fix this by turning positioning into:
a clear sales narrative
consistent messaging across channels
practical tools for teams to use


Positioning is not finished when it is written.
It is finished when it is adopted.
