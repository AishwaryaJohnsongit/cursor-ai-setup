Post 1 (Make Copy Concrete, Not Abstract)

Link: https://www.marketingexamples.com (Marketing Examples – core framework)
Content:
Most marketing fails because it uses abstract language.
Words like:


“better”


“faster”


“smarter”


“innovative”


sound nice, but they don’t create an image in the reader’s mind.
Good copy is concrete.
Instead of saying:
“Our product helps you get fit”
You say:
“Go from couch to 5K in 8 weeks”
Concrete language works because:


people can visualize it


it feels real and believable


it creates emotional clarity


If your copy cannot be pictured, it will not be remembered.
The goal is not to sound clever.
The goal is to be instantly understood.

Post 2 (Talk Less About Product, More About Outcome)
Link: https://www.marketingexamples.com
Content:
Most companies make the same mistake in their marketing.
They talk too much about themselves.


“We are the leading platform for…”


“Our AI-powered solution helps…”


“We provide end-to-end services…”


But customers don’t care about that.
They care about outcomes.
Strong copy shifts the focus from the product → to the result.
Instead of explaining what you are, you show:


what changes for the user


what problem disappears


what life looks like after


Example:
Instead of “a productivity app”
Say “finish your work by 4pm and stop working weekends”
Good marketing doesn’t describe the product.
It describes the transformation.
