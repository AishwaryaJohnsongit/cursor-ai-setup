Post 1 (People-first Marketing Thinking)
Date: ~recent
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_if-you-only-read-one-post-about-marketing-activity-7401961657884897280-xjeR 
Content:
Most marketing fails because it focuses on the wrong thing.
People do not care about your product.
They do not care about your features, your roadmap, or your company story.
They care about themselves.
The only question customers are asking is:
“What’s in it for me?”
Good marketing is not about talking more about your product.
It is about clearly answering:


How does this make my life easier?


How does this make me look better?


How does this save me time or money?


How does this reduce risk or pain?


If you cannot answer those clearly, nothing else matters.
The strongest marketing always starts with the customer’s reality—not the company’s message.

Post 2 (Brand, Attention & Modern SaaS Marketing)
Date: ~recent
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_10-lessons-from-10-years-of-interviewing-activity-7354091736438091776-1r64 
Content:
Modern B2B marketing is not just about demand capture anymore.
It is about building a brand that earns attention over time.
If you are invisible, you cannot compete—even if your product is great.
The companies that win consistently do a few things well:


They take a clear point of view


They communicate in a way people remember


They move fast and ship consistently


They build trust through repetition, not one-off campaigns


In crowded markets, being “good” is not enough.
You have to be:
distinct
memorable
consistent

Because people only buy from brands they recognize and trust.
Marketing is not just about leads.
It is about being remembered when the buyer is ready.
